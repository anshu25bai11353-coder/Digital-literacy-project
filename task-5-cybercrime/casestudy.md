# Case Study: Phishing Attack

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers pretend to be legitimate organizations or trusted individuals to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, bank details, or UPI credentials.

---

## How It Happens (Step by Step)

### Step 1: The Bait
The victim receives an email, SMS, or WhatsApp message that appears to be from a trusted source such as a bank, Amazon, Flipkart, or even a college. The message often uses the company's logo and formatting to look authentic.

### Step 2: Creating Urgency
The message creates a sense of urgency or fear. Common examples:
- "Your account will be blocked in 24 hours"
- "You won ₹50,000! Claim now"
- "Your order failed. Click here to fix"
- "Your UPI account needs verification"

### Step 3: The Fake Link
A link is provided that looks real but has small spelling mistakes. Examples:
- Real: `www.amazon.com`
- Fake: `www.amaz0n.com` or `www.amazon-update.com`

### Step 4: Fake Website
Clicking the link takes the victim to a website that looks identical to the real one. The URL might even show a padlock icon (which only means the connection is secure, not that the site is legitimate).

### Step 5: Data Theft
When the victim enters their login ID, password, OTP, or credit card details, the information is instantly captured by the attacker.

### Step 6: Fraud
The attacker uses this information to:
- Steal money from bank or UPI accounts
- Hack social media and email accounts
- Commit identity theft
- Sell the data to other criminals

---

## Realistic Scenario

**Victim:** Rahul, a 19-year-old college student in Delhi

**What Happened:**
Rahul received an SMS claiming to be from his bank: "Your account will be deactivated. Click here to verify: hxxp://sbi-verify[.]com"

Worried, Rahul clicked the link. The website looked exactly like SBI's login page. He entered his username, password, and the OTP received on his phone.

Within minutes, ₹25,000 was transferred from his account to an unknown UPI ID.

**What Went Wrong:**
- Rahul did not check the URL carefully
- He did not verify with the bank directly
- He shared OTP without realizing

## Who is Targeted?

| Target | How They Are Targeted |
|--------|----------------------|
| College Students | Fake internship offers, scholarship scams, prize alerts |
| Bank Customers | Account suspension threats, KYC update scams |
| Online Shoppers | Fake order delivery issues, refund scams |
| Job Seekers | Fake job offers asking for payment or documents |
| Senior Citizens | Fake calls from "bank officials" or "police" |

## Consequences

| Type | Impact |
|------|--------|
| Financial Loss | Money stolen from bank accounts, UPI fraud |
| Identity Theft | Personal information used for fraud, loans taken in victim's name |
| Account Hacking | Email, social media, and other accounts compromised |
| Emotional Distress | Anxiety, fear, loss of trust in digital platforms |
| Reputation Damage | Hacked accounts used to scam friends and family |

## Real Statistics

- India reported over **1.5 million cybercrime cases** in recent years
- **Phishing attacks increased by 60%** during the pandemic
- **College students (18-25)** are among the most targeted age group
- **UPI fraud** accounts for nearly 50% of digital payment frauds

---

## How to Protect Yourself

1. **Never share OTP** - No bank or company will ever ask for your OTP
2. **Verify the sender** - Check email addresses carefully (e.g., `@bank.com` vs `@bank-security.com`)
3. **Don't click links** - Type the official website URL directly in your browser
4. **Check for spelling errors** - Fake websites often have typos
5. **Use 2FA** - Enable two-factor authentication on all important accounts
6. **Report immediately** - If you become a victim, call 1930
